KickClip Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 11, 2026
Last Updated: May 11, 2026

Chrome Web Store Limited Use Disclosure
The use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

1. What KickClip Does (Single Purpose)

KickClip is a Chrome extension with a single purpose: to enable users to save images and social media posts from any webpage with a keyboard shortcut (Cmd+Shift+S on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+S on Windows/Linux), and to automatically organize saved items by content type (Img or SNS).

2. What We Collect, How, and Why

KickClip only collects data when you take a deliberate action (pressing the save shortcut, clicking the context menu item, or explicitly signing in). KickClip does not monitor your browsing activity, track pages you do not save, or run analytics in the background.

2.1 Account Information

  • What: Your Google account's unique identifier (UID), email address, display name, and profile picture URL.
  • When collected: When you sign in via the KickClip side panel.
  • How collected: Through Chrome's chrome.identity API using OAuth 2.0 with PKCE. KickClip does not store passwords.
  • OAuth scopes requested: openid, email, profile, and https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.file.
  • Why: To identify you as the owner of your saved items and to sync items across devices.
  • Where stored: Firebase Authentication (Google Cloud).

2.2 Saved Item Metadata

  • What: URLs, page titles, image URLs, content category (Img or SNS), domain name, timestamps, and the organizational folder you assigned (if any) for items you explicitly save.
  • When collected: Only when you press the save shortcut or click the context menu item on a page.
  • How collected: Via a content script that runs only at the moment of save, reading the active page's URL, title, and (if you saved an image) the image URL.
  • Why: To store and organize your clips so you can find them later in the KickClip side panel.
  • Where stored: Firebase Firestore (Google Cloud, Asia Northeast region), under your user account.

2.3 Saved Files

  • What: The actual image or content files you explicitly save.
  • When collected: Only when you press the save shortcut.
  • How collected: Downloaded from the source URL by KickClip.
  • Why: To preserve the saved content for your later access.
  • Where stored (you choose per save destination setting):
    • Local folder: A folder on your computer that you select using Chrome's File System Access API. KickClip writes saved files directly to this folder and cannot access other folders.
    • Google Drive: A folder named kickclip_files automatically created in your Google Drive root. KickClip uses the drive.file OAuth scope, which means KickClip can only access files KickClip itself creates — not your other Drive files.

2.4 User Preferences

  • What: Your save destination choice (local folder vs. Google Drive), UI toggles, and a reference to your selected local folder (if any).
  • How collected: Via chrome.storage.local.
  • Why: To remember your settings between sessions.
  • Where stored: Locally on your device only. Not transmitted anywhere.

3. What We Don't Collect

  • We do NOT track your browsing history.
  • We do NOT collect data from pages you do not explicitly save.
  • We do NOT use third-party analytics or trackers.
  • We do NOT sell or share your data with advertisers.
  • We do NOT use AI services to process your data or pages.
  • We do NOT collect sensitive content (e.g., email contents, passwords, financial data, health data).

4. Permission Justifications

KickClip requests the following Chrome permissions and uses them as follows:

  • storage — To store your preferences (save destination, UI settings) via chrome.storage.local on your device only.
  • downloads — When you choose a local folder as your save destination, to download saved files to that folder.
  • scripting — To inject the metadata-extraction content script into the active tab at the moment you press the save shortcut. Not used to monitor pages otherwise.
  • tabs — To read the active tab's URL and title at the moment you save, and to coordinate messages between the side panel UI and content scripts.
  • contextMenus — To add a "Save with KickClip" entry to Chrome's right-click menu as an alternative to the keyboard shortcut.
  • windows — To manage the lifecycle of KickClip's auxiliary windows (folder picker, popup notifications).
  • sidePanel — To display KickClip's main UI in Chrome's side panel, where you browse saved items by category.
  • identity — For Google sign-in via OAuth 2.0 to identify your account and request access to your Drive (only with drive.file scope).
  • clipboardWrite — To automatically copy the saved item's URL to your clipboard after saving, so you can immediately paste it elsewhere.

Host Permissions (<all_urls>)

KickClip requests permission to run on any URL because it is a universal save tool that must work on any website you choose to save from. Restricting to specific sites would defeat the core purpose.

Despite the broad URL pattern:

  • KickClip only acts in response to your explicit save shortcut press or context menu click.
  • KickClip reads only the metadata necessary for saving (URL, title, primary image, hovered element).
  • KickClip does not monitor your browsing activity in the background.
  • KickClip does not collect personal data from pages you do not save.
  • KickClip does not run continuous content analysis on visited pages.

The extension is functionally inert until you take a deliberate save action.

5. Data Sharing

KickClip shares your data only with the following service providers, strictly to operate the extension's documented functionality:

5.1 Google (Firebase — Authentication and Firestore)

  • What is shared: Your account information and saved item metadata.
  • Purpose: Backend infrastructure that hosts your account and saved items.
  • Subject to: Google's privacy policy and Google Cloud Platform Terms of Service.

5.2 Google Drive (only if you choose Drive as save destination)

  • What is shared: The files you explicitly save.
  • Purpose: Storage of your saved files in your own Google Drive.
  • Access scope: drive.file only — KickClip can only see and modify files KickClip itself created. KickClip cannot see your other Drive files.

KickClip does NOT share your data with any other third party. We do not sell, rent, or transfer your data to advertisers, data brokers, or analytics providers.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Saved items: Retained until you delete them individually (using the delete button on each item in the side panel) or delete your account.
  • Account data: Retained until you request account deletion.
  • Local data (chrome.storage.local): Cleared when you uninstall KickClip or use Chrome's extension settings to clear extension data.

To request account deletion, email tlstlsgns@gmail.com.

7. Your Rights

  • Access: View all your saved items via the KickClip side panel.
  • Delete individual items: Use the delete button on each item card.
  • Delete account: Email tlstlsgns@gmail.com requesting account deletion.
  • Revoke OAuth access: Remove KickClip's permissions at any time via Google Account Security Settings.
  • Local data: Use chrome://extensions to remove KickClip and clear local data.

8. Security

  • All network communication uses HTTPS (TLS encryption).
  • OAuth tokens are managed via Chrome's secure chrome.identity API.
  • Backend infrastructure (Firebase) uses Google Cloud Platform's industry-standard security practices.
  • Firebase Security Rules enforce per-user data isolation; one user cannot access another user's data.
  • KickClip itself does not store passwords. Authentication is delegated entirely to Google's OAuth flow.

9. Children's Privacy

KickClip is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If we become aware of such collection, we will delete the data immediately.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected in the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We will not retroactively change the policy to expand data collection beyond what was disclosed at the time of your saves.

11. Open Source

KickClip is open source. You can review the entire codebase at github.com/tlstlsgns/KickClip to verify the data handling described in this policy.
